Orange almond cake!

My own recipe for a moist, orange cake with a carmelized almond crust, and an orange almond icing.


***

Ingredients

For the cake:

• 1 1/2 cups sugar
• 1 3/4 flour
• 1 whole orange
• 1 1/2 teaspoons baking soda
• 1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
• 1 teaspoon salt (I like to use coarse)
• 2 teaspoons vanilla
• 2 eggs
• 1/2 almond milk (almonds + water, blended)
• 1/2 cup vegetable oil
• 1 cup hot orange juice

For the candied almond crust:

• 1 cup brown sugar
• 2 cups almonds, sliced (conserve half a cup for later)
• vanilla and salt to taste
• 1-2 cups water

For the dried oranges:

• 1 orange with peel, sliced
• sugar and salt for sprinkling

For orange almond icing:

• 1/2 cup almonds
• 1/2 cup orange juice
• orange peels (saved from the juiced orange)


***


Baking and making

The cake:

1. Preheat the oven to 350° (F)

2. Grease and flour a bundt cake pan

3. Mix the dry ingredients

4. In the blender, blend the whole orange with the almond milk until smooth.

Add the mixture along with eggs and vanilla to the dry ingredients, and mix until combined.

5. While mixing, pour in the hot orange juice. Mix well, and pour into the prepared cake pans.

Bake for 30-35 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.

The candied almonds:

1. While the cake is baking, bring one cup of water and the brown sugar to a boil on the stove. Add more water as needed
Lower the stove to medium/low, and stir the mixture until it reaches a caramel thickness.

2. Add in the almonds, vanilla, and salt. mix well, and pour onto a parchment-lined tray or plate, and place in the fridge or freezer to cool.

The dried oranges:

1. After the cake has been removed, set the oven to 400. 2. Lay the oranges on a parchment-lines baking tray.

3. Generously sprinkle brown sugar onto each slice, and finish with a lighter sprinkling of salt.

4. Bake for 5-10 minutes, until the orange sliced are darkening and dried.

The icing:

1. In the microwave, heat the orange juice with the orange peels for 1-2 minutes to help soften the peel.

2. Blend the orange juice, orange peel, and almonds in the blender until smooth.

The assembly:

Once the cake has cooled, you may begin assembling your cake!

1. Start by pouring the icing on: I like to pour it in a zig-zag pattern around the cake's center, to allow the icing to drip down it's sides.

2. After pouring the icing, get the cooled almonds. Using your hands or a spoon, take clumps of almonds and push them along the bottom edge of the cake, forming a "crust" of sorts around it's base.

3. Remember that half cup of almonds you saved? Sprinkle them freely on top of your cake. Any extra can be placed into the middle.

4. Arrange the dried oranges in a ring on top of the cake.

5. And last, but most certainly not least, enjoy!
